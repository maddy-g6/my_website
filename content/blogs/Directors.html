IMDB is an online database of information related to films, television programs, home videos, video games, and streaming content online â€“ including cast, production crew and personal biographies, plot summaries, trivia, ratings, and fan and critical reviews. Utilising IMDB data, I want to determe whether Steven Spielberg or Tim Burton has a higher mean rating. To do this, I have constructed visualisations and ran a hypothesis test.

First, we constructed a table of summary statistics as well as boxplots of both directors mean ratings to gain a better understanding of our dataset.

director mean_rating sd_rating count t_crit se margin_of_error ci_low ci_high
Steven Spielberg 7.57 0.695 23 2.07 0.145 0.301 7.27 7.87
Tim Burton 6.93 0.749 16 2.13 0.187 0.399 6.53 7.33

To test for a difference in the means, I used a t-test with the following hypotheses:

  • Ho: The mean rating for Steven Spielberg and Tim Burton is the same
  • H1: The mean rating is not the same
## 
##  Welch Two Sample t-test
## 
## data:  rating by director
## t = 3, df = 31, p-value = 0.01
## alternative hypothesis: true difference in means is not equal to 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  0.16 1.13
## sample estimates:
## mean in group Steven Spielberg       mean in group Tim Burton 
##                           7.57                           6.93

The t-test resulted with a t-statistic of 3 and a p-value of .01, meaning that we reject the null hypothesis. This means that there is statistically significant evidence that Steven Spielberg has a higher mean rating than Tim Burton.
